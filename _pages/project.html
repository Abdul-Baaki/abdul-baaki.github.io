Exploring Advanced NOMA Solutions for 6G Networks: Efficiency, Fairness, and Adaptation

Description

This project delves into cutting-edge NOMA (Non-Orthogonal Multiple Access) technologies to address critical challenges in 6G networks. By comparing NOMA with RSMA (Rate-Splitting Multiple Access), assessing the impact of path loss, and exploring Cooperative NOMA, the research aims to enhance multi-user communication. The project also investigates MIMO-NOMA, fairness, energy efficiency, and user pairing strategies, as well as the integration of link adaptation and SWIPT (Simultaneous Wireless Information and Power Transfer) to optimize performance.
